The 14-year-old dream.

With a growing passion in programming and the looming fear of making a career choice, it took one night and one game to change the course of my life for the better.

After completing The Last of Us and watching its "Making of," the stars aligned perfectly and I knew immediately that I was destined to be a game programmer.

Navigating the Unknown.

Treading in uncharted waters is scary, and unfortunately life got in the way making the lows extremely low. I lost almost all motivation. My grades dropped, the hope was lost, and the mountain seemed a bit too high to climb.

The passion never died, though, and playing The Last of Us Part 2 was the reassuring beacon in immense darkness. I had to start somewhere, anywhere, and after careful planning and a lot of elbow grease, I joined the University of London and started my Bachelor's in Computer Science.

Breaking Barriers.

Filled with determination, I set out to make a statement: I will be a game programmer one day. It was a mission of intent, meant to prove to myself that I'm capable. But little did I know how much University of London cared, and how much they noticed.

My story and hard work led me to becoming a Student Ambassador, a member of the Student Voice Group, and a Student Blogger. I was featured on multiple UoL podcasts and was becoming "the game development guy" amongst the university community.

Coursera was so inspired they flew me out and made a commercial based on my story. I remember calling my dad that day and laughing with him about me "becoming famous."
